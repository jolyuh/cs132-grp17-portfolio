Here's our problem formulation process.

Anakbayan is a Filipino youth-led organization that aims to mobilize the youth for national democracy [1]. Founded on November 30, 1998, the group is active in raising their voices about the Philippine government through activism.

In recent years, Anakbayan has been particularly vocal about their views on the two most recent presidents, Rodrigo Duterte and Ferdinand Marcos Jr., criticizing issues such as Duterte’s War on Drugs [2], Marcos’ Dynasty [3], and the Jeepney Modernization program launched by Duterte [4]. They have also expressed criticism towards Duterte’s measures to suppress the people, especially activists, such as the Anti-Terror Act he signed in 2020 [5] and the extravagant funding of the National Task Force to End Local Communist Armed Conflict (NTF-ELCAC) that was established in 2018 [6].

In retaliation, Anakbayan, along with other youth- and student-led groups, is vilified (i.e. red-tagged) by these administrations. They are often labeled as a terrorist communist group, being marked as a CPP-NPA front by state forces like the NTF-ELCAC [7] and antagonized under the public eye. Being red-tagged by the state like this is dangerous and leads to harassment and violence [8]. Moreover, both President Duterte and President Marcos Jr. are known to have a large number of very passionate and loyal supporters. This can be seen in the 2022 Philippine Elections, where the UniTeam were leading in the surveys [9] and ending up winning the elections by a large amount [10]. Hence, we would like to see whether the loyal supporters of these administrations are also spreading red-tagging allegations to Anakbayan.

  • Research Question

    Is there a correlation between the red-tagging of Anakbayan through tweets and being pro-Duterte or pro-Marcos?

  • Hypothesis

    Fanatics/apologists of Duterte are more likely to red-tag Anakbayan as a terrorist organization.

  • Alternative Hypothesis

    Fanatics/apologists of Marcos are more likely to red-tag Anakbayan as a terrorist organization.

  • Null Hypothesis

    Both fanatics and non-fanatics are equally likely to participate in the red-tagging of Anakbayan.

We mined the internet for fake news data.

Our data collection was conducted in two phases:

  • Phase 1: Initial data collection using a combination of manual search via Twitter's advanced search feature and scraping data with the assistance of Apify's Twitter Scraper actor. In this phase, we only collected tweets that contained red-tagging. Both standalone tweets and replies were included.
    • Number of tweets collected: 150
    • Date range of collected tweets: January 2016 to December 2022
    • Keywords: Anakbayan, NPA, CPP-NPA, communist, terrorist, komunista, terorista
  • Phase 2: Collection of additional data using the snscrape library. We collected tweets that mention Anakbayan in general during this phase. Just like the previous phase, standalone tweets and replies were both considered.
    • Number of tweets collected: 126
    • Date range of collected tweets: January 2020 to December 2022
    • Keywords: Anakbayan

The data features that we have notably added was data on their political leaning (whether they supported President Marcos and/or former President Duterte) by browsing their profile history, looking for any tweets, retweets, or likes in support of the said parties. A detailed rundown of our data features is below.

Data Features

  • Tweet Info Our data logs the tweet's contents, its metadata, and why we considered it to contain disinformation.
    • Date posted
    • Tweet contents
    • Type of tweet (Text, Image, Video, URL, Reply)
    • Content type (Rational, Emotional, Transactional)
    • Engagement (Likes, Replies, Retweets, Quote Tweets)
    • Reason for marking as disinformation
  • Account Info We also collected the account information of the respective posters of each tweet.
    • Account handle (@username)
    • Account name
    • Account bio
    • Account type (Identified, Media, Anonymous)
    • Date joined
    • Following
    • Followers
    • Location
  • Political Info We added flags to indicate the users' political affiliation and attitude towards Anakbayan.
    • Marcos supporter
    • Duterte supporter
    • Explanation for the political stance
    • Red-tagging
    • Tone (Positive, Negative, Neutral)


Data Preprocessing

To start off, we trimmed off the columns of our data that we wouldn't be needing to perform any analyses. These were the "meta" columns - columns like "ID", "Review", "Group". We only needed them for collaboration in the classroom.

Then, we cleaned up the data by going through all the null values. In particular, though, we didn't have to handle all null values because not all values are required nor available. For example, a Twitter account is not required to have a Location or an Account bio, which would mean those fields would be null in the dataset and that's normal. So in our inital search, only the following columns had null values that needed to be filled: Account name, Content type, Likes, Replies, Quote Tweets, and Explanation for the political stance. These values were re-checked and filled in place.

We also had to go through all the invalid entries. We had a problem with the Date posted field in particular. With datetime though, we standardized the entire column to be datetime objects instead of strings. Additionally, we decided to narrow the scope of our dataset to only tweets from 2020-2022, removing tweets that were posted before 2020.

Since our dataset had the values for political affiliation (Marcos supporter or Duterte supporter) and presence of red-tagging in the tweet as boolean values, we encoded our data to instead read these values as 1 for True and 0 for False.

Lastly, in order to prepare our dataset for the Chi-square test for independence, we created four mutually exclusive columns indicating their political affiliation. We initially tagged our data by checking both 'Marcos supporter' and 'Duterte supporter' columns if the user supports both, but since this is incompatible with one of the core assumptions of the Chi-square test, it was necessary to create these columns. These columns are also categorically encoded with True:1 and False:0. These columns are:

  1. supports_marcos_only
  2. supports_duterte_only
  3. supports_both
  4. supports_neither
